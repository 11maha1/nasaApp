In May 2024, the Sun exhibited intense solar activity, particularly between May 10 and 14, with NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO) capturing numerous solar flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs). These events resulted in some of the strongest solar storms recorded in two decades. During this period, several powerful X-class flares were detected, with the largest reaching X8.7 on May 14, 2024. The solar activity was so significant that it created widespread geomagnetic storms, resulting in brilliant auroras visible as far as the southern U.S. and northern India. The solar heat map of this period reflects the intense ultraviolet light emitted by these solar flares, highlighting extremely hot material in the Sun's corona
