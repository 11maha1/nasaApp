The impact of solar storms

Earth

- Communication Disruption: Solar storms can interfere with satellites, GPS, and radio communications. - Power Grid Issues: They can cause fluctuations in electrical systems, potentially leading to power outages. - Auroras: Increased geomagnetic activity produces stunning auroras in polar regions.

venus

- Atmospheric Effects: Venus has a thick atmosphere, and solar storms can enhance atmospheric escape, leading to changes in its upper atmosphere. - Radiation Exposure: Increased radiation levels from solar storms may affect any potential missions or probes studying the planet..

Mercury

- Surface Impact: As Mercury lacks a significant atmosphere, solar storms can directly impact its surface, causing changes in surface temperatures and releasing material. - Magnetic Field Interaction: Solar storms can interact with Mercury’s weak magnetic field, potentially affecting its surface conditions.

The Moon

- Radiation Exposure: Solar storms increase radiation levels in space, which can impact the Moon's surface. While the Moon has no atmosphere to protect it, this exposure could be a concern for future lunar missions. - Surface Changes: High-energy particles from solar storms can interact with the lunar regolith (the Moon’s surface layer), potentially altering its composition and creating secondary radiation. - Electrostatic Charging: Solar storms can cause the Moon’s surface to become electrically charged, which might affect dust movement and create hazardous conditions for landers and rovers
